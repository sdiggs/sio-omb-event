▲ Patterns worth a second look

  • Daytime dead zone. Zero comments carry a timestamp from 01:00 to 14:59 ET on any day. Organic submission usually spreads across working hours.
  • Repeating evening bursts. 3,749 of 46,025 (8%) landed on Jun 15, the busiest of 24 evening bursts across the window.
  • Same-second batching. 45,517 records share their exact second with at least one other, across 13005 seconds. The densest hold 7 each (e.g. 17:25:56, 17:26:04, 17:26:07, 19:49:23).
  • Form letters came later. The Jun 1–2 volume is overwhelmingly individual comments; the largest templated letters first appear Jun 3–5, so the early surge looks organic rather than coordinated.

Details

  • These are posting times, not submission times. regulations.gov often batch-posts a queue. The dead zone and same-second bursts fit an automated submitter, but they fit an agency posting on a schedule just as well. This data can't separate the two.
  • Stance is an estimate, not a hand count. Stance is estimated from the comment text by keyword, not hand-coded. 6,419 comments (14%) did not match either side cleanly; spot-checks show those are almost all opposition or attachment-only, so the oppose count is a floor.
