What the opposition argued

The 25 most common distinct stances against the proposed rule, drawn from 32,907 substantive opposition letters (excluding the pro-rule template and attachment-only comments). Ranked by how many letters raise each, with the opening of a representative letter for each.
How these were extracted Each stance is detected by matching argument-specific language across the opposition corpus, then counting how many distinct letters raise it. Percentages are of the 32,907-letter corpus and sum to more than 100 because most letters make several arguments. Keyword detection is approximate: it can miss a point made in unusual wording and group loosely related phrasings, so read the ranking as the shape of the opposition, not an exact tally. Excerpts are verbatim openings, lightly cleaned of markup; commenters' own spelling is left as written.
